Sustainability

What exactly is sustainability? The Oxford dictionary states that sustainability is "The property of being environmentally sustainable; the degree to which a process or enterprise is able to be maintained or continued while avoiding the long-term depletion of natural resource." In simpler terms, sustainability is ability to maintain or support a process over time. Sustainability is a very relevant issue in not only the computing industry but in all of society.

Sustainability is often broken down into 3 main concepts: economic, social and environmental.

Economic

Economic sustainability refers to pratices that support long-term economic growth. Initially, it may seem this is all about ensuring an organizations ability to keep making money, however it is so much more than this. An economically sustainable organizations achives this without negatively impacting social, environmental and cultural aspects of the community.

Social

In terms of businesses, social sustainability is a process of managing impacts of systems, processes and activities on people and the places they live and work. The impacts can be positive or negative, the crucial part is that they are both considers in the changes needed to be made by the business. Some things organizations can do to improve social sustainability are, cultivating a healthy work-life balance, emphasizing the importance of health and safety and prioritizing employee rights.

Environmental

This pillar focuses on safeguarding the environment for the next generation. Sustainable organizations aim to improve efficiency, minimize resource use and waste, and monitor carbon emissions throughout their supply chain. In order to do this, organizations are now using advanced technology, such as cloud and IoT sustainability solutions, to track and decrease their environmental footprint.
